# Is This Business Big Enough to Need This System?

> Before starting, decide which steps you actually need.
> Not every business idea needs all 7 steps.

```
Ask yourself: "How much of my life will this business consume?"

  Small side income (₹10-50K/month extra)
  → Skip Steps 1, 2, 3. Go straight to Step 5.
  → Example: Freelancing, tutoring, small service

  Medium business (₹1-10L/month, your main work)
  → Start at Step 3. Use Steps 3 → 4 → 5 → 6 → 7.
  → Example: Agency, product brand, local business

  Big business (₹10L+/month, building a company)
  → Use ALL steps in order 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 6 → 7.
  → Example: SaaS, manufacturing, recycling empire

  Life-changing empire (₹1Cr+/month, multi-year journey)
  → Use all steps. Break into phases.
  → Run this system once per phase.
  → Example: Infrastructure play, platform business
```

**Decision Table:**

| Situation | Steps to Use |
|---|---|
| Quick side income, already know what to do | Step 5 → Step 6 → Step 7 |
| Know the sector, need to pick a business | Steps 3 → 4 → 5 → 6 → 7 |
| Know nothing, exploring what business to start | All steps 1 → 2 → 3 → 4 → 5 → 6 → 7 |
| Already chose a business, need execution plan | Steps 5 → 6 → 7 |
| Business running but stuck or failing | Bonus prompts only |

# Should I Even Start This Business?

> Use this BEFORE spending any time or money.
> This prompt tells you honestly whether to proceed and why.
> The most important prompt in the entire system.

---

## Should I Start This — Prompt

```
I am considering starting a business in [SECTOR / IDEA].

My current situation:
  - I currently have: [skills, capital available, time per week,
    location, existing network or experience]
  - My income goal is: [how much you want to earn and by when]
  - My lifestyle goal is: [freedom, stability, empire, side income]
  - My timeline is: [how soon you need first revenue]
  - I am considering this because: [why you think this is right]

Tell me honestly:

1. Is [BUSINESS IDEA] the RIGHT choice for me given my situation?
   If not, what should I pursue instead and why?

2. What specific advantages do I have that make this more likely
   to succeed for ME specifically versus someone else?

3. What are the PREREQUISITES I need before starting?
   Capital, skills, licenses, network — do I have them?
   What am I missing and how long to get it?

4. What is the MINIMUM viable version of this business I can
   test in 30 days? What can I skip entirely at the start?

5. What is the realistic income timeline for someone in my
   exact situation? Month 1, Month 6, Year 1, Year 3.

6. What is the single biggest reason this could FAIL for me
   specifically? Not generic risks — MY specific risks.

7. Give me a final verdict:
   START NOW / START LATER / DO NOT START THIS
   With a one paragraph explanation of why.
```

# What Do I Already Have?

> Answer these honestly before running Step 1.
> Your answers change which steps you need.

```
Answer honestly before starting:

  1. Do I have experience in this sector?
     YES → Skip Step 1 and Step 2. Go to Step 3.
     NO  → Start at Step 1.

  2. Do I know who the competitors and players are?
     YES → Skip Step 2. Go to Step 3.
     NO  → Do Step 2 industry map.

  3. What is my primary constraint right now?
     No capital         → Look for easy entry businesses only
     No time            → Look for businesses that run part-time
     No skills          → Look for businesses that match what I know
     No network         → Look for businesses with no relationship need
     No location        → Look for digital or remote businesses

  4. What is my primary goal?
     First income fast  → Step 5, get 30-day action plan immediately
     Build empire       → All steps in order, do not skip
     Replace job        → Steps 3 → 4 → 5, focus on income replacement
     Invest capital     → Step 3, filter by capital requirements

  5. Do I already know which business I want?
     YES → Go straight to Step 5 — Deep Dive.
     NO  → Start at Step 1 or Step 3.

  6. Am I doing this alone or with a partner?
     Alone   → Note this in every prompt, affects what is feasible
     Partner → Use Find Co-Founder bonus prompt first
```

# Step 1 — Universe Map

> **Purpose:** See every business that exists across all sectors before committing.
> Use this when you have no idea what business to start.
> Skip if you already know which sector interests you.

---

## Step 1 Prompt — Copy and paste this

```
I want to understand every type of business that exists
so I can pick the right one for my situation.

My situation:
  - Skills I have: [list your skills]
  - Capital available: [how much money you can invest]
  - Location: [city and country]
  - Time available: [hours per week]
  - Current income: [monthly income you need to maintain or replace]

Show me:

1. Every major sector and category of business that exists
2. For each sector:
   - Entry difficulty (easy / medium / hard)
   - Capital required to start
   - Time to first revenue
   - Key challenges
   - Who it is best suited for

3. Organize by entry difficulty — easiest first

4. For each sector, give 3-5 specific business examples

5. At the end, show me a dependency chain:
   Which businesses feed into which other businesses
   Which businesses are recession-proof
   Which businesses have government tailwind right now

6. Based on MY situation above, highlight the top 10
   businesses that match my constraints the best.

Do NOT go deep on any single business yet.
Just show me the complete map so I can see what exists
before I commit to any direction.
```

---

## What You Get Back

Every sector, every type of business, organized by difficulty with your top 10 matches highlighted. You now see the full universe before choosing.

## What to Do After Step 1

```
Are there sectors that immediately excite you?
  YES → Note them. Take maximum 3 into Step 2.
  NO  → Ask: "Which of these sectors has the most
        government tailwind in [COUNTRY] right now?"
        That answer narrows your choices.

Is the universe bigger than you expected?
  YES → Use Score and Filter in Step 3 to narrow down.
  NO  → Move to Step 2 with your shortlist.
```

# Step 2 — Industry Map

> **Purpose:** Understand a specific sector deeply before entering it.
> Who are the players, what are the rules, what is missing.
> Use this for your top 1-3 sectors from Step 1.
> Run this prompt once per sector.

---

## Step 2 Prompt — Copy and paste this

```
I am exploring [SECTOR] as a potential business.
I know almost nothing about this industry.

Before I decide to enter, show me the complete industry map:

1. PLAYERS — Who are the major players?
   - Large established companies (with revenue if known)
   - Mid-size growing companies
   - Small players doing well
   - Recent startups that raised funding
   - Which companies dominate this space in [COUNTRY]

2. MONEY FLOWS — How does money move in this industry?
   - Who pays whom
   - What are the margins at each level
   - Where is the most money concentrated
   - Which part of the chain is most profitable

3. REGULATIONS — What rules govern this industry?
   - Key licenses and permits required
   - Government bodies involved
   - Recent regulatory changes in last 2-3 years
   - Upcoming regulations that will change the industry

4. GAPS — What is missing in this industry?
   - What do customers complain about
   - What do small players struggle with
   - What technology has not been adopted yet
   - What problem nobody has solved well

5. TIMING — Is now a good time to enter?
   - Government schemes or subsidies available
   - Industry growing or shrinking
   - What tailwinds exist right now
   - What headwinds to watch out for

6. ENTRY POINTS — Where can a new player enter?
   - Easiest entry point for someone with [MY SKILLS/CAPITAL]
   - Which part of the chain is least competitive
   - What angle would be hardest to copy

Show this as a complete industry reference.
This is my due diligence before committing.
```

---

## What You Get Back

A complete picture of the industry — who controls it, where money flows, what is missing, where you can enter. You now understand the industry before spending a rupee.

## What to Do After Step 2

```
Does the industry make sense to enter given your situation?
  YES → Move to Step 3 to score and filter specific businesses.
  NO  → Go back to Step 1 and pick a different sector.

Did you find a clear gap nobody is solving?
  YES → Note it. This becomes your positioning in Step 5.
  NO  → The opportunity may be in execution not innovation.
        Doing existing businesses better is a valid path.
```

# Step 3 — Score and Filter

> **Purpose:** Narrow down from many options to your top 3 using a scoring system.
> Use this when you have 5-20 business ideas and cannot decide.
> This removes emotion from the decision.

---

## Step 3 Prompt — Copy and paste this

```
I have a list of businesses I am considering.
Help me score and filter them to find my best option.

My situation:
  - Skills: [your skills]
  - Capital available: [amount]
  - Time available per week: [hours]
  - Income needed by Month 6: [amount]
  - Location: [city]
  - Risk tolerance: [low / medium / high]
  - Goal: [first income / replace job / build empire]

Businesses I am considering:
  1. [Business 1]
  2. [Business 2]
  3. [Business 3]
  [add more]

Score each business on these 8 criteria (1-5 points each):

  EASY TO START    → How quickly can I get first revenue?
  SAFE             → How low is the failure risk?
  SCALABLE         → How big can this realistically get?
  SPEED            → How fast is first income?
  MY ADVANTAGE     → Does my specific background help here?
  RECESSION-PROOF  → Does demand survive economic downturns?
  GOVERNMENT HELP  → Are there subsidies, tailwinds, mandates?
  CAPITAL FIT      → Does it match my available capital?

For each business show:
  - Score out of 40
  - The one biggest strength
  - The one biggest risk for ME specifically
  - Honest probability of success for someone in my situation

End with:
  - Top 3 ranked with clear reasoning
  - Which ONE to start first and why
  - What the top business would look like in Year 1 and Year 3
```

# Step 4 — Pick Your Path

> **Purpose:** Match the right empire path to who you are.
> Your skills and personality determine which path has the highest
> probability of success. The wrong path for your profile fails
> even with perfect execution.

---

## Step 4 Prompt — Copy and paste this

```
I have narrowed my business options to [TOP 1-3 FROM STEP 3].
Help me pick the right path based on who I am.

About me:
  - Current profession: [your job or background]
  - Skills I am confident in: [technical, sales, operations, creative]
  - Skills I am weak in: [be honest]
  - My personality: [introvert/extrovert, detail/big picture,
    risk-taker/risk-averse, builder/seller]
  - What I enjoy doing: [the actual work, not the outcome]
  - What drains me: [work I hate doing]
  - People I know: [industry contacts, investors, etc.]

For each of my top options, tell me:

1. PATH FIT — Does this path match my profile?
   What will come naturally to me here?
   What will feel like a constant struggle?

2. ROLE I WOULD PLAY — In this business, am I:
   The builder? The seller? The operator? The strategist?
   Is that the role I actually want?

3. CO-FOUNDER NEED — What kind of person would I need
   alongside me to cover my weaknesses?
   Can I do this solo or must I have a partner?

4. ENERGY MATCH — Will I still want to work on this
   in Year 3 when it is hard and slow?
   Or will I quit when it stops being exciting?

5. REALISTIC OUTCOME — For someone exactly like me,
   what is the realistic Year 5 outcome for each path?
   Not the best case — the most likely case.

End with:
  One clear recommendation of which path fits me best.
  The reason it fits better than the others.
  The one thing I must watch out for on this path.
```

# Step 5 — Deep Dive

> **Purpose:** Get the complete guide for your chosen business.
> Everything you need to know before spending money or quitting your job.
> This is the main reference document for your chosen path.
> Generate in parts for complex businesses.

---

## Step 5 Prompt — Copy and paste this

```
I have chosen to build [BUSINESS NAME / TYPE].
Give me the complete deep dive on this business.

My profile:
  - Background: [your profession and skills]
  - Capital to invest: [amount]
  - Time available: [hours per week, full-time or part-time]
  - Location: [city]
  - Goal: [income target and timeline]

Cover ALL of the following:

PART 1 — HOW THE BUSINESS WORKS:
  - Exactly how money is made (revenue model)
  - The complete chain from start to customer payment
  - What I do every day in this business
  - What a typical week looks like in Month 1 vs Year 1

PART 2 — REAL NUMBERS:
  - Exact startup costs (list every expense)
  - Monthly operating costs
  - Revenue per customer or per transaction
  - How many customers needed to break even
  - Realistic Month 1, Month 6, Year 1, Year 3 revenue
  - What the best small players in this business actually earn

PART 3 — PLAYERS AND COMPETITION:
  - Who are my direct competitors in [MY CITY]
  - What do they do well
  - Where are they weak
  - How do I position against them
  - Is there room for a new player right now

PART 4 — LICENSES AND COMPLIANCE:
  - Every license and permit required
  - Which ones I need before Day 1 vs can get later
  - Exact cost and time for each
  - Which government bodies are involved
  - Common compliance mistakes that get businesses shut down

PART 5 — GETTING FIRST CUSTOMERS:
  - Who exactly is my first customer (describe them precisely)
  - Where do I find them
  - How do I approach them
  - What do I say to make them pay
  - What the first sale conversation looks like

PART 6 — SCALING:
  - How does this business grow from 1 customer to 100
  - What breaks first as I scale
  - When to hire the first person and what role
  - What Year 3 looks like if I execute well

PART 7 — BIGGEST MISTAKES:
  - Top 5 mistakes people make when starting this business
  - The mistake that kills this business most often
  - What successful players did differently in Year 1

PART 8 — EMPIRE PATH:
  - Where can this business go in 10-15 years
  - What does the empire version look like
  - Which businesses does this lead into naturally
  - Real examples of people who built this path

FORMAT:
  - Single markdown document
  - Clickable table of contents
  - Back to contents after every section
  - Real numbers, not ranges where possible
  - Generate in parts — Part 1 and 2 first,
    then wait for me to say "Generate Part 2"
```

---

## What to Do After Step 5

```
Does the business still make sense after seeing the real numbers?
  YES → Move to Step 6 — 30-Day Action Plan.
  NO  → Go back to Step 4 and pick a different path.
        Better to learn this now than after spending money.

Are there things in the deep dive you do not understand?
  YES → Use the I Am Stuck bonus prompt.
  NO  → Move to Step 6.

Are there licenses you do not have?
  YES → Note them. Your first week includes getting these started.
  NO  → Move to Step 6.
```

# Step 6 — 30-Day Action Plan

> **Purpose:** Exact day-by-day actions from today to first revenue.
> No vague advice. No "do research." Specific actions with specific outcomes.
> This is what you actually do starting tomorrow morning.

---

## Step 6 Prompt — Copy and paste this

```
I have decided to start [BUSINESS NAME].
I need an exact 30-day action plan to get my first paying customer.

My situation:
  - Starting from: [zero customers / have some contacts / etc.]
  - Capital I can spend in first 30 days: [amount]
  - Hours available per day: [number]
  - Doing this: [full-time / evenings and weekends]
  - My city: [city]

Give me a day-by-day plan:

FORMAT FOR EACH DAY:
  Day [N]:
  → Main task: [the one most important thing]
  → Secondary task: [supporting action]
  → Goal for today: [what done looks like]
  → Expected outcome: [what you have at end of day]

WEEK 1 (Days 1-7): Foundation
  - What to set up
  - What to register or apply for
  - Who to call or visit
  - What to prepare before talking to customers

WEEK 2 (Days 8-14): First Conversations
  - How many people to contact per day
  - What to say in first conversation
  - How to handle the most common objection
  - What a successful conversation looks like

WEEK 3 (Days 15-21): First Customers
  - How to close the first sale
  - What to do when someone says no
  - How to get referrals from early conversations
  - What to do if nobody is responding

WEEK 4 (Days 22-30): First Revenue
  - How to get from conversation to payment
  - What to do after first customer pays
  - How to get second and third customer immediately
  - What the end of Day 30 should look like

Also tell me:
  - The ONE thing that determines if Month 1 succeeds or fails
  - The most common reason people fail in their first 30 days
  - What to do if I am at Day 20 with zero customers
  - What success looks like at Day 30 (specific numbers)
```

# Step 7 — Execute and Review

> **Purpose:** Track what is working, fix what is not, know when to pivot.
> Run the monthly review every 30 days for the first year.
> Run the quarterly review every 3 months.

---

## Step 7 Prompt — Monthly Review

```
I have been running [BUSINESS NAME] for [NUMBER] months.
Help me review what is working and what to do next.

What happened this month:
  - Revenue earned: [amount]
  - Customers acquired: [number]
  - Customers lost: [number]
  - Money spent: [amount]
  - Hours worked: [number]
  - Biggest win: [what went well]
  - Biggest problem: [what went wrong]

What I tried:
  - [Action 1 and result]
  - [Action 2 and result]
  - [Action 3 and result]

What I avoided or did not do:
  - [Thing I kept putting off and why]

My honest assessment:
  - Am I on track for my original goal? [YES / NO / PARTIALLY]
  - My energy level with this business: [high / medium / low]
  - The one thing that would change everything: [describe it]

Tell me:

1. DIAGNOSIS — Based on my numbers, what is the real situation?
   Am I growing, stalling, or declining?

2. WHAT IS WORKING — What should I do MORE of?
   Which action gave the best results?

3. WHAT IS NOT WORKING — What should I STOP doing?
   What am I spending time on that is not producing results?

4. NEXT 30 DAYS — What are my top 3 priorities?
   What is the single most important thing to fix or double down on?

5. SHOULD I CONTINUE — Honest answer:
   Is this business worth continuing at this pace?
   What would have to be true in 60 days to keep going?
```

---

## Step 7 Prompt — Quarterly Strategy Review

```
I have been running [BUSINESS NAME] for [NUMBER] months.
Time for a deeper strategy review.

Current state:
  - Monthly revenue: [amount]
  - Monthly profit: [amount]
  - Number of active customers: [number]
  - Biggest customer segment: [describe]
  - What I spend most time on: [describe]
  - What customers say they value most: [describe]

Original goal was: [what you planned to achieve by now]
Actual position is: [where you actually are]

Tell me:

1. IS THIS STILL THE RIGHT BUSINESS?
   Given what I know now that I did not know at the start,
   is this still the best use of my time and capital?

2. WHAT HAS CHANGED?
   What assumptions were wrong?
   What surprised me positively and negatively?

3. THE NEXT PHASE:
   What does the next 6 months need to look like?
   What is the one capability I must build that I do not have?
   What should I stop, start, and continue?

4. EMPIRE CHECK:
   Am I still on the empire path I chose?
   Do I need to adjust the long-term plan?

5. RESOURCE ALLOCATION:
   Am I spending time and money on the right things?
   What is the highest leverage action available to me right now?
```

## Validate Before Committing Prompt

> Use BEFORE spending money or quitting your job.
> Tests if real people will actually pay — not just say they like the idea.

```
I want to validate whether [BUSINESS IDEA] has real demand
before I invest significant time or money.

My idea in one sentence:
[Describe what the business does and who it is for]

My assumptions:
  - I assume customers have this problem: [describe problem]
  - I assume they will pay: [price you plan to charge]
  - I assume I can reach them through: [channel you plan to use]
  - I assume the biggest competitor is: [who you think competes]

Tell me:

1. What is the fastest and cheapest way to test each assumption?
   Give me a specific test for each one.

2. What does REAL validation look like vs false validation?
   People saying "great idea" is NOT validation.
   What is actual proof of demand?

3. Give me a 2-week validation plan:
   - Day 1-3: [what to do]
   - Day 4-7: [what to do]
   - Day 8-14: [what to do]

4. What numbers tell me the idea is validated?
   What numbers tell me to change direction?

5. What is the minimum version I can sell before building anything?
   Can I take money first and deliver later?

The goal is to find out if this works
before spending significant time or money.
```

## I Am Stuck Prompt

> Use when you hit a specific wall and do not know how to get past it.
> Works for any stage — finding customers, operations, pricing, hiring.

```
I am stuck on [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] in my business [BUSINESS NAME].

Here is my situation:
  - Stage of business: [how long running, revenue level]
  - What I have tried: [list what you already attempted]
  - What happened when I tried: [results of each attempt]
  - What I think the real problem is: [your hypothesis]

Here is the specific thing blocking me:
[Describe in detail what you cannot figure out]

Help me by:
  1. Diagnosing the real root cause
     Is this the actual problem or a symptom of something deeper?

  2. Giving me 3 different approaches to solve it
     Not the obvious one I probably already thought of

  3. Telling me which approach you would try first and why

  4. Showing me a real example of how someone solved
     this same problem in a similar business

  5. Telling me what I should NOT do
     The tempting wrong solution that makes this worse

Do not give generic advice.
Give me specific actions for MY specific situation.
```

## Find Co-Founder or Partner Prompt

> Use when you realize you cannot do this alone.
> Helps you define exactly who you need and how to find them.

```
I am building [BUSINESS NAME] and I need a co-founder or partner.

My strengths:
  [List what you do well]

My weaknesses that are hurting the business:
  [List where you are struggling because of skill gaps]

What the business needs that I cannot provide:
  [Sales, technical, operations, industry connections, capital, etc.]

Tell me:

1. PROFILE — What exact type of person do I need?
   Skills, experience, personality, network.
   Be specific — not "a good salesperson" but
   "someone who has sold B2B compliance software to Indian enterprises."

2. EQUITY — What is a fair equity split for this situation?
   What factors determine whether it is 50/50, 60/40, or 70/30?
   What vesting schedule should we use?

3. WHERE TO FIND — Where do I actually find this person?
   Specific platforms, communities, events, and strategies.

4. HOW TO APPROACH — What do I say?
   Give me a message template for reaching out.
   What questions do I ask to screen them?

5. RED FLAGS — What to avoid?
   Signs of a bad co-founder match before it is too late.

6. ALTERNATIVE — If I cannot find the right person,
   what is the best way to handle this without a co-founder?
   Hire, outsource, partnership, advisory equity?
```

## Raise Money Prompt

> Use when you need outside capital to grow.
> Covers what type of funding fits your stage and how to get it.

```
I need to raise money for [BUSINESS NAME].

Current state:
  - Monthly revenue: [amount or zero]
  - Monthly growth rate: [percentage]
  - How long running: [months]
  - What I have built so far: [describe]
  - Number of customers: [number]
  - Capital needed: [amount]
  - What the money is for: [specific use]

Tell me:

1. WHAT TYPE OF FUNDING FITS MY STAGE?
   Bootstrap / Revenue-based / Angel / Seed VC /
   Bank loan / Government grant / Customer advance?

2. AM I READY TO RAISE?
   What would investors look for at my stage?
   What am I missing that will make them say no?

3. WHO TO APPROACH?
   Specific names of investors, funds, or programs
   relevant for my sector and stage.
   What government schemes apply to my business?

4. THE PITCH:
   What is my one-line pitch?
   What are the 5 things every investor needs to see?
   What questions will they definitely ask?

5. TERMS TO UNDERSTAND:
   What is a fair valuation for my stage?
   What terms should I never agree to?

6. ALTERNATIVE TO RAISING:
   Can I grow without outside money?
   What is the cost of raising vs not raising?
```

## I Am Failing Prompt

> Use when revenue is not growing, customers are leaving,
> or you are close to giving up.

```
My business [BUSINESS NAME] is not working.

Here is the honest situation:
  - Running for: [months]
  - Revenue: [current monthly revenue]
  - Was supposed to be: [what you planned by now]
  - Customers: [how many active]
  - Cash remaining: [how long you can continue]
  - What I have tried: [everything you have done]
  - What is not working: [describe specifically]

My gut feeling about why it is failing:
[What you think is wrong — even if you are not sure]

Tell me honestly:

1. DIAGNOSIS — Is this a fixable problem or a fundamental flaw?
   Is this bad business or bad execution of a good business?
   Be direct. I need truth not encouragement.

2. IF FIXABLE — What specifically needs to change?
   What exact action in the next 14 days could change trajectory?

3. IF NOT FIXABLE — What does an honourable exit look like?
   How do I wind down without destroying relationships?

4. PIVOT OPTIONS — Is there an adjacent version of this
   business that has a better chance?

5. THE QUESTION I NEED TO ANSWER:
   Keep going / Pivot / Stop?
   Give me a framework for making this decision
   based on my specific numbers and situation.
```

## What Should I Do Next Prompt

> Use when the current phase is working and you need to plan the next phase.

```
My business [BUSINESS NAME] is at this stage:
  - Monthly revenue: [amount]
  - Growth rate: [monthly percentage]
  - Customers: [number]
  - Team: [just me / number of people]
  - Main product or service: [describe]
  - What is working well: [describe]
  - What is consuming most of my time: [describe]

I am considering these next moves:
  Option A: [describe]
  Option B: [describe]
  Option C: [describe]

Tell me:

1. STAGE DIAGNOSIS — What stage is my business at?
   Validation / Early traction / Growth / Scale / Mature?
   What are the typical moves at this stage?

2. OPTION ANALYSIS — For each option I listed:
   - What does it unlock?
   - What does it cost in time and money?
   - What is the risk if it does not work?
   - Probability it works for my specific situation?

3. WHAT I AM MISSING — Is there an option I have not considered
   that would be higher leverage than all three I listed?

4. PRIORITY ORDER — In what order should I pursue these?

5. THE ONE THING — If I could only do one thing in the next
   90 days to move the business forward the most,
   what would it be and why?
```

## Emergency Revenue Plan Prompt

> Use when you have a hard deadline — runway ending, bills due,
> need revenue in days not months.

```
I need to generate [AMOUNT] in revenue within [NUMBER] days.

My business: [BUSINESS NAME]
My situation:
  - Current monthly revenue: [amount]
  - What I sell: [describe your product or service]
  - Existing customers: [how many, what they pay]
  - Leads or conversations in progress: [describe]
  - Resources I have: [time, team, product ready, contacts]

This is urgent because: [describe why you need this by this date]

Give me:

1. FASTEST PATH TO [AMOUNT] — What is the single fastest
   way to generate this revenue given my specific assets?
   Not the best long-term move — the fastest move.

2. DAY BY DAY PLAN:
   Day 1: [exactly what to do]
   Day 2: [exactly what to do]
   ... until target date

3. WHO TO CALL TODAY — Based on what I have described,
   who are the 5 most likely people to pay me quickly?
   What do I say to each of them?

4. WHAT TO OFFER — Is there a version of my product or
   service I can offer right now that closes faster?
   Smaller scope, upfront payment, special bundle?

5. IF I MISS THE TARGET — What is the backup plan?
```

  BUILD: [what building it would involve]
  BUY or HIRE: [what paying for it would look like]
  PARTNER: [who could I partner with and how]

1. WHICH OPTION IS RIGHT for my stage and situation?

2. EXACT COST AND TIME for each option realistically.
   Not best case — realistic case.

3. WHAT I AM NOT SEEING — Is there a fourth option
   I have not considered?

4. THE RISK OF EACH — What breaks if I choose wrong here?

5. WHAT TO DO THIS WEEK — If I choose the best option,
   what are the first 3 actions?

## Competitor Analysis Prompt

> Use before entering a market or when a competitor is hurting you.

```
I need to understand my competitors for [BUSINESS NAME].

My business:
  - What I sell: [describe]
  - Who I sell to: [describe customer]
  - My location or market: [city / online / national]
  - My price point: [amount]

Competitors I know about:
  1. [Competitor name and what you know]
  2. [Competitor name and what you know]
  3. [Competitor name and what you know]

Tell me:

1. COMPETITOR MAP — Are there competitors I am not aware of?
   Include direct, indirect, and future competitors.

2. WHAT THEY DO WELL — What are they genuinely good at?
   Why do customers choose them?

3. WHERE THEY ARE WEAK — What do their customers complain about?
   What do they consistently fail to deliver?

4. MY POSITIONING — Given their strengths and weaknesses,
   where is the clearest gap for me to own?
   What position should I take that they cannot easily copy?

5. HOW TO TALK ABOUT THEM — What do I say when a customer
   asks "how are you different from [Competitor]?"
   Give me a specific, honest, compelling answer.

6. LONG-TERM — In 3 years, how does the competitive landscape
   change? Who wins and why?
```

## Pricing Strategy Prompt

> Use when you are not sure what to charge, or when pricing is not working.

```
I need to figure out the right pricing for [BUSINESS NAME].

What I sell:
  [Describe your product or service in detail]

Who buys it:
  [Describe your customer — size, type, budget, pain level]

Current situation:
  - What I charge now or plan to charge: [amount]
  - Customer reaction to price: [describe]
  - My cost to deliver: [amount]
  - Competitors charge: [what you know]
  - Conversion rate: [percentage of people who say yes]

The problem I am facing:
  [Too few buying / margins too thin / unsure what to charge /
   customers negotiating too much]

Tell me:

1. PRICE DIAGNOSIS — Is my current pricing too high, too low,
   or wrong in structure?

2. WHAT PRICING MODEL FITS — Which model suits this business?
   Fixed / Per-unit / Subscription / Value-based /
   Retainer / Commission / Tiered?

3. THE RIGHT NUMBER — What should I charge?
   Give me a specific number with reasoning.

4. HOW TO RAISE PRICES — If I am undercharging,
   how do I increase prices without losing existing customers?
   Give me the exact conversation to have.

5. PACKAGING — Should I offer tiers or bundles?
   How do I make the middle option the obvious choice?

6. WHAT TO SAY — When a customer says "too expensive",
   what do I say? Give me word-for-word responses.
```

# Quick Cheat Sheet

## The Full System at a Glance

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                             │
│  BEFORE STARTING                                            │
│  Should I Even Start This? → Validate before committing     │
│  What Do I Already Have?   → Pick the right starting step   │
│                                                             │
│  STEP 1 — Universe Map                                      │
│  "What businesses exist? Show me everything."               │
│  → Skip if you already know which sector                    │
│                                                             │
│  STEP 2 — Industry Map                                      │
│  "Who are the players, where is money, what is missing?"    │
│  → Run once per sector you are seriously considering        │
│                                                             │
│  STEP 3 — Score and Filter                                  │
│  "Score my options and tell me which fits me best."         │
│  → Removes emotion from the decision                        │
│                                                             │
│  STEP 4 — Pick Your Path                                    │
│  "Does this path match who I actually am?"                  │
│  → Wrong path for your profile fails even with execution    │
│                                                             │
│  STEP 5 — Deep Dive                                         │
│  "Give me the complete guide for my chosen business."       │
│  → Generate in parts for complex businesses                 │
│                                                             │
│  STEP 6 — 30-Day Action Plan                                │
│  "Exact day-by-day plan to first paying customer."          │
│  → Specific actions with specific outcomes                  │
│                                                             │
│  STEP 7 — Execute and Review                                │
│  "What is working, what is not, what next?"                 │
│  → Monthly review every month for first year                │
│  → Quarterly strategy review every 3 months                 │
│                                                             │
│  BONUS PROMPTS                                              │
│  Validate First       → Test demand before spending money   │
│  I Am Stuck           → Specific problem, specific solution │
│  Find Co-Founder      → Who you need and how to find them   │
│  Raise Money          → Type, who to ask, how to pitch      │
│  I Am Failing         → Honest diagnosis before giving up   │
│  What Should I Do     → Next phase when current one works   │
│  Emergency Revenue    → Fast money when deadline is real    │
│  Build vs Buy         → Right way to get a capability       │
│  Competitor Analysis  → Understand them before positioning  │
│  Pricing Strategy     → What to charge and how to structure │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Which Step Am I On?

```
Never started, no idea what business?       → Step 1 Universe Map
Know the sector, need to understand it?     → Step 2 Industry Map
Have multiple ideas, cannot decide?         → Step 3 Score and Filter
Chose a business, does it fit who I am?     → Step 4 Pick Your Path
Ready to go deep on chosen business?        → Step 5 Deep Dive
Know what to do, need day by day plan?      → Step 6 Action Plan
Running the business, need to review?       → Step 7 Execute and Review
Not sure if I should even start?            → Should I Even Start prompt
Hit a specific wall?                        → I Am Stuck prompt
Nobody is buying?                           → Validate prompt
Need money fast?                            → Emergency Revenue prompt
Business not growing?                       → What Should I Do Next prompt
Business losing money or customers?         → I Am Failing prompt
Need a skill or person I do not have?       → Co-Founder or Build vs Buy prompt
Not sure what to charge?                    → Pricing Strategy prompt
Competitor taking my customers?             → Competitor Analysis prompt
```
