# Two Scenarios

**Your solo path (guaranteed, no dependency on anyone):** You follow the Hybrid Path alone. US remote job for income. Build recycling tech platform on evenings. This happens regardless of whether your friend agrees or not. This is your Plan A that never changes.

**Your optional multiplier (if friend agrees):** Your AC technician friend follows the Scrap to Empire path physically. You partner with him. You now have two businesses — one digital, one physical — that feed each other. This is your Plan B that makes Plan A even more powerful.

# How to Pitch

**Do NOT show him the full 1,111-line document.** That will overwhelm him just like 582 businesses overwhelmed you. Instead, tell him this:

---

> There is a business where you go to the scrap market with ₹10,000, buy copper at ₹610 per kg, sell at ₹660 per kg, and make ₹800-1,500 profit in 2-3 days. No cold selling. No security guards. No convincing anyone. Both buyer and seller want to deal with you. You scale this to ₹2-5 lakh per month within 8 months. Then you specialize in electronic scrap where margins are 3-5 times higher. Then you set up a dismantling facility. Then battery recycling. Companies like Attero started exactly like this and are now worth $300 million. The entire path is documented step by step — what to do on Day 1, Day 2, Day 3, all the way to Year 15.

---

**Why this pitch works for an AC technician specifically:**

| What He Already Knows | How It Connects to Scrap Trading |
|---|---|
| Works with his hands every day | Scrap market rewards physical work — sorting, grading, loading |
| Understands copper — AC units have copper coils and tubes | Copper is the highest-margin scrap metal. He can identify grades by touch. |
| Understands aluminum — AC units have aluminum fins | Aluminum is the second most traded scrap metal. He already knows it. |
| Dismantles things for a living | E-waste dismantling is literally his existing skill applied to a bigger market |
| Comfortable in workshops and industrial settings | Scrap markets and recycling facilities will feel natural, not intimidating |

This is not asking him to learn something new. This is an extension of what he already does, but in a market with 10 times the growth potential.

**What to tell him about the partnership:**

You handle technology, digital marketing, compliance software, and eventually build a platform that other recyclers will pay for. He handles physical operations — buying, sorting, processing, facility management. You both put skin in the game. You both benefit from both businesses growing.

# Partnership Terms

## His Physical Recycling Business

| Term | Detail |
|---|---|
| His ownership | 70-80% |
| Your ownership | 20-30% |
| His role | Runs operations daily — buying, sorting, processing, selling, facility, workers |
| Your role | Technology support, IndiaMART listing, simple tracking tool, EPR brand approaches on LinkedIn |

## Your Tech Platform

| Term | Detail |
|---|---|
| Your ownership | 70-80% |
| His ownership | 20-30% |
| Your role | Build the platform — design, development, marketing, fundraising |
| His role | First customer, primary feedback source, daily operational data, industry introductions |

## Non-Negotiable

| Requirement | Why |
|---|---|
| Written agreement signed by both | Not a handshake. A legal document. |
| Covers: who owns what percentage | Prevents future "I thought I owned more" disputes |
| Covers: what happens if one person wants to exit | Prevents being locked in a bad partnership forever |
| Covers: how profits are shared | Prevents money fights that destroy friendships |
| Lawyer cost: ₹10,000-25,000 | The most important document in the partnership. Worth every rupee. |
