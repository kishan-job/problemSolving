## How To Read This Document

This document answers one question: where in India does a real problem exist, money is being lost or risk is being taken, and no good software has been built yet?

It is structured in two layers.

Part 1 gives you the framework — 6 different types of triggers that create software opportunities. Understanding the type tells you how hard the sell is, what urgency looks like, and what risks you face.

Part 2 gives you the specific problems — 8 real unsolved situations across 6 sectors, each checked against whether a funded player already exists.

The EPR recycling opportunity is intentionally excluded. That analysis is in the separate Reality Check document. The lesson from EPR — Recykal arrived years before enforcement got serious — is applied throughout. Every problem here has been checked for an existing Recykal-equivalent before being included.

# Part 1 — The 6 Types of Opportunity

The EPR blueprint used one specific trigger — government compliance pressure. There are actually 6 different types of triggers that create the same pattern: large industry, real pain, no software yet. The type determines how easy it is to sell, how urgent the customer feels, and what risks you face.

---

## Type 1 — Government Compliance Pressure

*The EPR pattern. A new regulation exists. Businesses must comply or face fines, shutdown, or criminal liability. The deadline creates urgency no salesperson could manufacture.*

### How It Works

```
Government passes rule
        ↓
Businesses must comply by a deadline
        ↓
Old method — Excel, paper, consultant — breaks under scrutiny
        ↓
They need software. Not because they want it.
Because the alternative is shutdown.
        ↓
You sell survival, not convenience.
```

### Industries Where This Trigger Exists Now

No Recykal-equivalent has arrived in any of these yet:

| Industry | Regulation Trigger | Stage | Urgency |
|---|---|---|---|
| FSSAI food compliance | New audit and labelling rules 2024 | Early — no funded player | Medium, growing |
| Textile EPR | Rules expected 2026-27 | Very early — nobody exists | Low now, high soon |
| Construction waste | Debris management rules 2024 | Early — zero funded player | Low-medium |
| Gems hallmarking HUID | Mandatory since 2021, silver added Sept 2025 | Mid — weak tools only | High right now |
| Pesticide lot tracking | New rules forcing lot-level tracing | Early | Medium |
| Clinical establishment reporting | New NHSRC mandates | Early | Low-medium |
| Schedule M pharma GMP | Revised drugs rules 2023, deadline Jan 2026 | Early — no player | Extremely high now |
| Consent to Operate renewal | SPCB rules, always existed but never digitized | Early — ignored for decades | Medium, consistent |
| CBAM carbon for exporters | EU regulation effective 2026 | Very early — no India SME player | High and growing |

### Why EPR Failed But This Type Still Works

EPR failed because Recykal arrived in 2019 — 7 years before enforcement got serious. Every row in the table above has no equivalent of Recykal yet. The window is open. The risk is that someone else notices before you build.

### Sell Motion for Type 1

You do not need to convince anyone of the problem. The government already did that. Your pitch is one sentence: "You got a notice. Our platform means you never get another one."

## Type 2 — Operational Pain Without Compliance Pressure

*The industry is making money but operations are genuinely broken. No regulation forces change. They pay because they are losing time, money, or customers — not because the government threatens them.*

### How It Works

```
Business is profitable
        ↓
But: job cards on paper, inventory in notebook,
     follow-ups by memory, invoices typed manually
        ↓
Owner knows it is broken but survives anyway
        ↓
Pain threshold crossed when business grows
or when a competitor with software pulls ahead
        ↓
They pay to fix the pain — when they are ready
```

### Industries Where This Trigger Exists

| Industry | The Specific Pain | Customer Count | Willingness to Pay |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standalone auto service centres | Job cards paper, parts inventory notebook, customer follow-up memory | 5,000+ garages in Hyderabad alone | ₹2,000-8,000/month |
| Chartered accountant firms | Client documents on WhatsApp, deadlines in Excel | 1.2 lakh CA firms in India | ₹3,000-10,000/month |
| Wedding vendors | Booking conflicts, payment tracking, coordination all WhatsApp | 50 lakh+ vendor businesses | Low-medium |
| Offset printing presses | Job tracking, paper stock, delivery — all manual notebooks | 2 lakh+ printing businesses | Low-medium |
| Cold storage facilities | Temperature logs paper, client inventory Excel | 8,000+ cold storages | ₹5,000-15,000/month |
| Kirana supply chain | Distributor orders via WhatsApp voice notes | Very crowded — many players | Avoid |

### Key Difference From Type 1

There is no deadline forcing adoption. The sell is harder. The customer says "we managed without it for 20 years." You need to show a direct money connection — time saved per day multiplied by cost of that time, or customers lost per month due to no follow-up system.

### Best Customer Profile for Type 2

A business that is growing and recently hired 5-10 staff. At 2 people the owner manages everything in their head. At 10 people the old system breaks completely. That breaking point is your entry moment.

## Type 3 — Trust Gap Between Two Parties

*Two parties in a transaction don't fully trust each other. Both have information the other needs but won't share honestly. Software acts as a neutral third party that both sides can trust.*

### How It Works

```
Party A and Party B transact regularly
        ↓
Each side has incentive to misrepresent something
        ↓
Disputes happen — expensive, relationship-destroying
        ↓
A neutral digital record both parties sign
eliminates the misrepresentation
        ↓
Both sides pay because both benefit from the trust layer
```

GST itself solved a trust gap — buyer and seller both report the same transaction to the government so neither can cheat. This is the same principle applied to private transactions.

### Where Trust Gaps Exist in India Today

```
Landlord ↔ Tenant
→ Rental agreements, maintenance requests, security deposit disputes
→ No neutral platform — both sides misrepresent
→ 5 crore+ rental units in India
→ No good software exists

Builder ↔ Home Buyer
→ Construction progress claims unverifiable
→ RERA exists but no software implements it well
→ Builder says "60% complete" — buyer cannot verify
→ Crores of under-construction units affected

Freelancer ↔ Client (Indian domestic market)
→ Scope creep, payment disputes, no paper trail
→ Upwork solves this for US clients — not Indian domestic
→ Millions of domestic freelancers unprotected

Patient ↔ Hospital
→ Medical records owned by hospital, not patient
→ Patient moves city — records are lost
→ ABDM health ID exists but no good consumer interface

Farmer ↔ Mandi Buyer
→ Weight manipulation, price opacity at point of sale
→ Farmer has no proof, buyer has no accountability
→ e-NAM exists but field implementation is weak
```

### The Chicken-And-Egg Risk

Type 3 requires both sides to adopt. If only landlords sign up it has no value. If only tenants sign up it has no value. You must think carefully about which side to acquire first and why the other side will follow automatically.

## Type 4 — Coordination Problem Across Fragmented Players

*An industry has hundreds or thousands of small independent businesses that must coordinate with each other to get work done. Currently this happens through phone calls and WhatsApp. Software that coordinates them creates value for everyone.*

### How It Works

```
20 sub-contractors working on one building project
        ↓
Main contractor calls each one every morning
WhatsApp groups with 200 messages per day
Nobody has a single clear picture
Material arrives at wrong site. Attendance disputed.
        ↓
A platform where all parties update their own data
gives the main contractor a real-time single view
        ↓
Main contractor pays. Sub-contractors join because
they must to get work from the main contractor.
```

### Where Coordination Problems Exist in India

```
Sub-contractors in Construction
→ Main contractor managing 20 sub-contractors
→ Daily attendance, material usage, progress — all WhatsApp
→ No software for the small main contractor (₹5-50Cr projects)
→ Large contractors have SAP. Small ones have nothing.

B2B Freight (not Ola/Uber — truck logistics)
→ 5 lakh small truck owners
→ Still coordinated by phone and broker networks
→ Porter and BlackBuck exist but Tier 2 and 3 cities have gaps

Event Management (mid-size)
→ Vendor coordination for 200-person corporate event
→ 15 vendors, all on WhatsApp, no single timeline view
→ No good tool for mid-size event companies

Healthcare Referral Network
→ GP refers patient to specialist
→ No digital referral trail
→ Specialist never sends back report to referring GP
→ GP has no idea what happened to their patient
```

### The Network Effect Risk

Type 4 winner takes all once network effects kick in. If someone else builds it first and reaches critical mass, you cannot displace them even with a better product. Move fast once you identify a Type 4 opportunity.

## Type 5 — Paper Data That Needs Digitizing

*Valuable data exists in physical form — registers, files, notebooks. Whoever digitizes it first owns the insight layer that sits on top of it.*

### How It Works

```
Data exists physically — in registers, court files, farm records
        ↓
No one can search, analyze, or act on it at scale
        ↓
A buyer exists who would pay for that data digitally
        ↓
You build the digitization layer first
then build the insight layer on top
        ↓
Data network effect: more data = better insights
= harder for anyone to compete with
```

### Where Paper Data Is Trapped in India

```
District Court Records
→ Most district court documents still physical
→ Lawyers spend hours manually searching precedents
→ Supreme Court and High Court somewhat digitized
→ District level — almost nothing

Land Records
→ Digitized in metros, broken in rural areas
→ Title verification still requires physical search
→ One failed government attempt (DILRMP)
→ Banks, lawyers, buyers all need this

Agricultural Yield Data
→ State governments collect crop yield manually
→ Not machine readable, not aggregated nationally
→ Insurance companies, input companies, banks need this

Truck Weighbridge Data
→ Every highway weighbridge logs trucks on paper or DOS systems
→ Nobody aggregates into a national freight movement picture
→ Logistics companies would pay significant money for this
```

### Why Type 5 Is Hardest for a Solo Developer

Data digitization requires physical field operations — people visiting courts, government offices, farms. It requires capital before revenue. A solo developer cannot do this alone without funding. Avoid Type 5 until you have a team and investors.

## Type 6 — Behavior Change From External Shock

*Something changed in the world and an entire industry must adapt. The adaptation requires new software. Incumbents are slow because their existing product was built for the old world.*

### How It Works

```
External shock hits an industry
        ↓
Old software does not fit the new reality
        ↓
Businesses need new tools but existing vendors
are slow to rebuild because they have legacy customers
        ↓
A new product built specifically for the new reality
wins customers that incumbents cannot serve
        ↓
Window: 2-3 years before incumbents catch up
```

### Where External Shocks Have Created Gaps in India

```
EV Transition
→ Fleet operators switching from diesel to EV
→ Route planning changes completely
  (charge points, range anxiety, charging time windows)
→ Existing fleet software built entirely for diesel constraints
→ No fleet management tool built specifically for EV fleets
→ Ola, Ather, Tata EV commercial fleets need this now

ONDC — Open Network for Digital Commerce
→ Government-built protocol forcing commerce to be interoperable
→ Thousands of small retailers need ONDC-compatible presence
→ Most do not know how to set up or manage ONDC
→ No simple onboarding tool for the small kirana on ONDC

UPI Reconciliation for Business
→ Small businesses collecting UPI payments but reconciliation manual
→ No easy tool that maps UPI inflow to specific orders or invoices
→ CA firms manually reconcile this every month for thousands of clients
→ Particularly painful for businesses with 100+ UPI transactions per day

Hybrid Work Management (Indian context)
→ Indian companies managing hybrid attendance in Excel
→ No good Indian tool built for Indian labour law plus hybrid model
→ Global tools (Workday, BambooHR) too expensive and US-centric
→ Gap exists for mid-size Indian companies of 50-500 employees
```

### The Timing Risk in Type 6

The window closes faster than other types. Once incumbent vendors notice the gap they rebuild within 12-18 months. You must reach 50+ paying customers before the incumbent notices. After that, your existing customer relationships protect you.

## Which Type Fits A Solo React Developer Best

Given the profile — React developer, building alone, limited time, US remote job running in parallel:

**Best fit: Type 1 and Type 6**

Type 1 because the government creates urgency you do not have to manufacture. The customer is already scared. The sell is short. Price sensitivity is low when the alternative is shutdown.

Type 6 because the window is now. ONDC, EV fleets, and UPI reconciliation are 2026 problems. No incumbent has rebuilt for them yet. You can build and ship in 3-4 months and have paying customers before anyone notices.

**Second best: Type 2**

Harder sell but the customer is stable and predictable. CA firms and standalone garages are not going anywhere. Revenue is consistent once acquired. Good for a platform that compounds slowly.

**Avoid: Type 5**

Requires field operations and capital before any revenue. Impossible to build alone without leaving your job.

**Approach with caution: Type 3 and Type 4**

Both have structural risks — chicken-and-egg for Type 3, winner-takes-all for Type 4 — that a solo developer with limited time cannot navigate safely.

### Problem 1 — EPCG Obligation Tracking

**Opportunity Type:** Type 1 (compliance pressure) + Type 2 (operational pain)

#### What EPCG Is

Export Promotion Capital Goods scheme. A manufacturer who imports machinery for production can import it at zero or reduced customs duty. In exchange they must export a certain value of goods — typically 6 times the duty saved — within 6 years. If they fail, they must pay back the full duty plus 15% interest.

#### What The Real Problem Is

```
A factory in Hyderabad imported CNC machines in 2021
Duty saved: ₹80 lakhs
Export obligation: ₹4.8 crore worth of goods by 2027

Today in 2026:
→ How much have they exported against this obligation?
→ Which shipments qualify?
→ Are they on track?
→ What happens if they miss the deadline?

Answer: Nobody in the company knows precisely.

The data exists across shipping bills, GST records,
and DGFT portal entries — all in different places.
The owner checks with their DGFT consultant once a year
who manually compiles it and charges ₹50,000-2 lakh.

If they miss the deadline:
→ Pay back ₹80 lakh duty plus ₹12 lakh interest
→ Potentially blacklisted from future DGFT benefits
→ This happens more than anyone admits
```

#### Who The Customer Is

50,000+ manufacturers hold active EPCG licences across India. In Hyderabad — pharma, auto components, electronics manufacturing in HITEC City industrial areas. Every single one tracks this in Excel or relies on a consultant who checks annually.

#### What Software Needs to Do

- Pull shipping bill data from ICEGATE
- Pull DGFT authorisation data
- Calculate obligation fulfilled vs remaining automatically
- Alert at 90, 60, 30 days before deadline
- Generate redemption application documentation automatically

#### Why Nobody Built It

Specific enough that Tally and SAP did not bother. Generic enough that no startup noticed. Painful enough that manufacturers will pay ₹8,000-20,000/month to avoid the risk. The market is not glamorous — which is exactly why it is still open.

#### Revenue Potential

```
Target customers  : 50,000 manufacturers nationally
Realistic capture : 500 in Year 1, 2,000 in Year 3
Price             : ₹8,000-20,000/month
Year 1 MRR        : ₹40-100L
Year 3 MRR        : ₹1.5-4Cr
```

### Problem 2 — DGFT Scheme Eligibility Alerts for Small Exporters

**Opportunity Type:** Type 1 (compliance) + Type 2 (operational pain)

#### What The Real Problem Is

India's Foreign Trade Policy 2023 is a living document — new notifications come every 2-3 weeks changing duty drawback rates, RoDTEP rates, eligible products, and documentation requirements. A small garment exporter or spice exporter in Hyderabad qualifies for multiple schemes but misses benefits because their consultant checks once a year and notifications are in government PDF format — unreadable to a busy owner.

```
Real example:
Spice exporter in Guntur
Annual exports: ₹5 crore
RoDTEP benefit they qualify for: 4% of export value
Money they should get back: ₹20 lakh per year
Money they actually claimed: ₹8 lakh
Reason for gap: Consultant missed a notification
                that changed their product's eligibility rate
Loss: ₹12 lakh that year alone
```

#### Who The Customer Is

3 lakh+ small exporters registered with DGFT. Textiles, handicrafts, spices, pharma bulk drugs, engineering goods. Most use consultants who charge ₹1-3 lakh per year and are themselves not tracking daily notifications.

#### What Software Needs to Do

- Monitor DGFT notifications daily (publicly available)
- Map each notification to the exporter's product category and ITC-HS code
- Alert when a relevant change occurs
- Calculate estimated benefit impact in rupees
- Generate claim application pre-filled

#### Revenue Potential

```
Target customers : 3 lakh small exporters
Realistic capture: 1,000 in Year 1, 5,000 in Year 3
Price            : ₹2,000-5,000/month
Year 1 MRR       : ₹20-50L
Year 3 MRR       : ₹1-2.5Cr
```

### Problem 3 — Schedule M GMP for Pharma MSMEs

**Opportunity Type:** Type 1 — Government compliance pressure — extremely urgent right now

#### What Schedule M Is

Schedule M is the section of India's Drugs and Cosmetics Rules specifying Good Manufacturing Practices for pharmaceutical factories. In December 2023 the government revised it comprehensively. Large manufacturers had to comply by June 2024. Small and medium pharma manufacturers had until January 2026 — that deadline has now passed.

#### What The Real Problem Is

```
A small pharma unit in Patancheru, Hyderabad
Makes 15 generic drug formulations
Employs 40 people
Annual revenue: ₹3 crore

What Schedule M requires them to document:
→ Every batch: raw material source, quantity, test results
→ Every equipment: calibration date, maintenance log
→ Every process: SOP followed, deviations noted
→ Every worker: training records, health certificates
→ Environmental monitoring: temperature, humidity logs
→ Deviation reports when anything goes wrong
→ Annual product review for each formulation

What they actually do today:
→ Paper registers for batch records
→ Calibration dates written on sticky notes on machines
→ SOPs printed and laminated on wall — never updated
→ Drug inspector visits → owner and manager spend 3 days
  frantically compiling paper records into files

What happens when records are inadequate:
→ Warning letter from CDSCO
→ Stop production notice
→ In serious cases: licence cancellation
→ Entire business worth ₹5-20 crore destroyed
```

#### Scale of This Problem

Gujarat alone has 3,000 pharma manufacturing units, over 90% MSMEs. Hyderabad — Patancheru, IDA Bollaram, Genome Valley, Jeedimetla — has hundreds more. Total pharma MSMEs in India: 10,000+. Every single one faces this compliance burden. None have affordable software for it.

Existing options and why they fail:
- SAP Pharma: ₹50 lakh+ implementation. Impossible for MSME.
- Generic ERP (Tally): Does not understand pharma compliance structure.
- Paper: Getting rejected by drug inspectors increasingly.
- Consultants: ₹50,000-2 lakh per inspection preparation.

#### What Software Needs to Do

- Batch record entry with all mandatory Schedule M fields
- Equipment register with calibration alert system
- SOP management with version control
- Deviation logging and CAPA tracking
- Annual product review auto-generation
- Drug inspector visit preparation — one click to compile all records in required format
- CDSCO and SPCB submission format outputs

#### Why This Is The Strongest Opportunity In This Document

```
Location advantage
Hyderabad = India's second largest pharma hub
Patancheru, IDA Bollaram, Genome Valley, Jeedimetla
Hundreds of MSME pharma units within 30 km of you
You can walk in without travelling anywhere

Timing
Deadline passed January 2026
Drug inspectors visiting units RIGHT NOW — April 2026
Units receiving notices RIGHT NOW
This is current urgency, not upcoming urgency
Every week you wait is a week of missed opportunity

No competitor
Zero funded startup in this space
SAP costs ₹50 lakh+ — impossible for MSME
Generic ERP does not understand pharma compliance
Paper records getting rejected at inspections
Gap is completely open

Customer willingness to pay
Owner of ₹2-10Cr pharma unit understands risk personally
₹20,000-40,000/month is nothing vs ₹5Cr licence loss
This is survival spending not convenience spending

Sales motion
Walk into any pharma unit in Patancheru
Ask: "Have you received an inspection notice recently?"
Answer will be yes
Show demo on laptop
Close same week
No long enterprise sales cycle
Owner decides on the spot
```

#### Revenue Potential

```
Target customers  : 500 units in Hyderabad alone
Realistic capture : 50 in Year 1, 200 in Year 2
Price             : ₹15,000-30,000/month
Year 1 MRR        : ₹75L-1.5Cr
Year 2 MRR        : ₹3-6Cr
```

A construction project worth ₹20 crore has one main contractor and 15-25 sub-contractors — civil, electrical, plumbing, false ceiling, painting, HVAC, lifts. Every day the main contractor must know who showed up, what material was consumed, what progress was made, what issues are blocking whom, and what payments are due.

The gap is mid-size main contractors:

```

#### What Software Needs to Do

- Daily attendance marking by sub-contractor supervisors via mobile
- Material issue and consumption tracking per work package
- Progress percentage updates with photo evidence
- Payment milestone tracking against completed work
- Issue and delay logging with resolution tracking
- Main contractor dashboard across all projects in one view

#### Revenue Potential

```
Target customers  : Mid-size contractors, Hyderabad has hundreds
Price             : ₹10,000-25,000/month per contractor
Year 1 MRR        : ₹50-100L with 50 customers
```

### Problem 5 — Consent to Operate Renewal Tracking

**Opportunity Type:** Type 1 — Compliance pressure — consistent and permanent, not tied to a single deadline

#### What Consent to Operate Is

Every factory in India that generates air emissions, liquid effluents, or solid waste must have a Consent to Operate from the State Pollution Control Board. In Telangana this is TSPCB. Renewed annually for high-polluting units, every 5 years for lower-polluting ones. Renewal requires submitting updated pollution data, effluent treatment records, stack emission reports, and compliance certificates.

#### What The Real Problem Is

```
4 lakh+ factories hold active SPCB consents across India

What happens today:
→ Renewal date written in a notebook or forgotten entirely
→ Factory continues operating on expired consent unknowingly
→ SPCB inspection finds expired consent → notice issued
→ Renewal documents compiled manually in panic
→ Environmental consultant charges ₹20,000-1 lakh per renewal
→ If consent lapses significantly → criminal liability for owner

What does not exist anywhere:
→ Dashboard showing all consent expiry dates
→ Automatic reminders at 90, 60, 30 days before expiry
→ Document vault for all environmental compliance records
→ Auto-generated renewal application from stored data
→ SPCB submission format output
```

#### Why This Problem Is Underrated

This affects every factory regardless of what they make — pharma, textiles, chemicals, food processing, auto components. It is not tied to a single industry crisis. It is a consistent permanent pain that never goes away. Every customer you acquire stays for years because the consent renewal cycle never ends. Combine this with Problem 3 — pharma units need both — and you double revenue per customer.

#### Revenue Potential

```
Target customers  : Any factory in Telangana first, then national
Realistic capture : 200 units Year 1, 1,000 Year 3
Price             : ₹3,000-8,000/month
Year 1 MRR        : ₹6-16L
Year 3 MRR        : ₹30-80L
Combination play  : Bundle with Problem 3 pharma tool
                    Same customer, significantly higher revenue per account
```

### Problem 6 — CBAM Carbon Footprint Tracking for Indian Exporters

**Opportunity Type:** Type 1 (compliance) + Type 6 (external shock — EU regulation)

#### What CBAM Is

The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism requires that goods exported to the EU — specifically steel, aluminium, cement, fertiliser, and hydrogen — must now report the embedded carbon emissions in their production process. Indian exporters in these categories must calculate and report their product carbon footprint or their EU buyer pays an equivalent carbon price, making Indian goods more expensive and less competitive.

#### What The Real Problem Is

```
An Indian steel re-roller in Vizag
Exports ₹50 crore worth of steel sections to EU buyers annually

Since 2026, EU buyer asks for carbon footprint per tonne of steel

What the Vizag unit knows about their carbon footprint:
→ Nothing precisely
→ They know they use electricity and coal
→ They do not know how to calculate Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions
→ They do not know what methodology EU requires

### Problem 8 — HUID Inventory Management for Small Jewellers

**Opportunity Type:** Type 1 — Government compliance pressure — BIS mandate ongoing

#### What HUID Is

Hallmark Unique Identification. Since June 2021, every piece of gold jewellery sold in India must carry a 6-digit HUID code assigned at a BIS-approved Assaying and Hallmarking Centre. From September 2025, silver jewellery hallmarking became mandatory. From July 2025, 9K gold was added. BIS adds more categories every year. From 2025, a mandatory API integration means every step of the hallmarking process must be reported digitally to the BIS portal.

#### What The Real Problem Is

```
A small jeweller in Secunderabad with one shop
Monthly sales: 50-100 pieces of gold jewellery

What BIS requires:
→ Each piece purchased has a HUID
→ HUID recorded on purchase invoice
→ Inventory must show which HUIDs are in stock
→ When piece is sold, HUID linked to customer invoice
→ BIS stock verification team visits →
  physical inventory must exactly match HUID records

What the jeweller actually does:
→ Generic billing software records weight and description
→ No HUID recorded anywhere in the system
→ No link between HUID and inventory
→ BIS verification team visits → owner cannot produce records
→ Fine: up to ₹2 lakh per violation
→ BIS registration cancellation risk

Existing software options:
→ Generic billing (Vyapar, Tally): no HUID field at all
→ Jewellery ERP (Jewel Mate, etc.): expensive, complex,
  built for large jewellers with 10+ staff
→ BIS portal: government system, terrible UX, no inventory link

The gap:
Simple mobile-first tool for small jeweller that:
→ Scans HUID on purchase → auto-enters inventory
→ Scans HUID on sale → auto-links to customer invoice
→ Shows real-time inventory reconciled with HUID records
→ BIS verification visit: one button generates full report
```

#### Hyderabad Advantage

Hyderabad has one of India's largest jewellery clusters — Secunderabad, Abids, Dilsukhnagar, Kukatpally. Thousands of small jewellers within the city. Door-to-door demos possible in one weekend. First 10 paying customers findable without any digital marketing.
